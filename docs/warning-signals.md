# Warning Signals

> Your personal "dashboard warning lights." Learn to read them before the engine blows.

For high-sensitivity / high-focus individuals, **the "tired" signal is usually delayed**. When you feel tired, you've actually already over-depleted. You need to capture **earlier, fainter signals**.

---

## Why Early Detection Matters

You can't drive a Ferrari by waiting for smoke to come out of the hood. By the time you feel "tired," you've already overdrafted your account. The self-attack spiral is about to begin.

**The goal:** Catch the warning lights while you can still safely pull over.

## The "Check Engine" Questions

Ask yourself these every 90 minutes:

1. **Can I read this line of code on the first try?**
2. **Are my hands warm?**
3. **When did I last drink water?**
4. **Am I sighing without noticing?**
5. **Did anything minor just annoy me disproportionately?**

If you answer "no" or "yes" to the wrong ones, it's pit stop time.

## Building Your Personal Warning System

Everyone's signals are slightly different. Use the [Debugging Log](./debugging-log.md) to track your episodes and identify YOUR specific early warnings.

Common patterns people discover:
- "My left eye starts twitching about 20 minutes before crash"
- "I start craving sweet coffee specifically"
- "I notice I'm holding my breath"
- "My typing speed drops noticeably"

## The Meta-Warning

**The most dangerous signal: "I'm fine, just 10 more minutes."**

When this thought appears, you're already past warning into critical. The prefrontal cortex is rationalizing its own shutdown.

**Rule:** If you catch yourself negotiating for "just a bit more time," that IS the signal.
